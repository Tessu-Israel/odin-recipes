Lasagna

Randy's Slow Cooker Ravioli Lasagna

Though often associated with Italian cuisine, lasagna’s roots trace back to ancient Greece. The word "lasagna" comes from the Greek "lasagnum," meaning "chamber pot," which referred to a type of flat pasta baked in the oven.

Lasagna became widely popular in the United States thanks to Italian immigrants. They adapted the recipe using locally available ingredients, like ricotta instead of traditional Italian cheeses.

The traditional Italian lasagna, especially from Emilia-Romagna, is layered with ragù (meat sauce), béchamel (white sauce), and Parmesan cheese. Meanwhile, American versions often use ricotta, mozzarella, and tomato sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 bag (25 oz) frozen cheese or meat ravioli
  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara or pasta sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup ricotta or cottage cheese (optional)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Layer Sauce: Pour a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of your slow cooker.
  2. Add Ravioli: Place a single layer of frozen ravioli on top of the sauce.
  3. Cheese Layer: Add a layer of ricotta or cottage cheese (if using) and sprinkle some mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  4. Repeat Layers: Repeat the layering with sauce, ravioli, and cheeses until you’ve used up all the ingredients, ending with a final layer of sauce and mozzarella on top.
  5. Cook: Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 4-5 hours or on high for 2-3 hours, until the ravioli is tender and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Serve: Garnish with fresh basil or parsley if desired, and enjoy!
